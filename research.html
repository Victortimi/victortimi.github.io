Current Research

PhD Dissertation Focus

🔬 Active Research · ISEP Paris / Sorbonne University
Explainable Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models for O-RAN Self-Healing: Anomaly Detection, Root-Cause Analysis, and Knowledge-Grounded Remediation
Open Radio Access Networks (O-RAN) represent a paradigm shift in mobile network architecture, disaggregating previously monolithic systems into interoperable components. While this openness enables innovation, it also introduces complex fault scenarios that require intelligent, autonomous management. This research develops XAI-driven frameworks and LLM-powered systems that can detect network anomalies in real time, diagnose root causes through interpretable reasoning chains, and generate knowledge-grounded remediation strategies — all in a manner that network operators can understand, verify, and trust. The work sits at the intersection of AI transparency, natural language understanding, and telecommunications engineering.
