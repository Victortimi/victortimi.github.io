Biography

Who I Am

I am an Applied AI Researcher and Petroleum & Energy Engineer with over five years of experience spanning reservoir simulation, data engineering, and frontier AI research. My career has followed an unconventional but purposeful arc — from modelling oil reservoirs deep underground to designing intelligent systems for the telecommunications networks of tomorrow.

I bridge theoretical AI into industrial applications — developing explainable, knowledge-grounded systems that make complex industrial networks smarter, safer, and self-healing. In 2023, I was awarded a PTDF Overseas Scholarship to pursue an MSc in Data Science & Analytics at EPITA School of Engineering and Computer Science in Paris, where I relocated and also built robust ETL pipelines and data infrastructure alongside my academic work. I graduated in 2025, and was subsequently awarded a second PTDF PhD scholarship to continue at Sorbonne University / ISEP Paris within the EDITE doctoral school.

My current PTDF-funded doctoral research sits at the intersection of Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) and Large Language Models (LLMs), applied to making Open Radio Access Networks (O-RAN) autonomous and self-healing. AI systems must not only perform — they must explain themselves, reason over structured knowledge, and act in ways engineers can understand and trust. I am a two-time recipient of the prestigious PTDF Overseas Scholarship, and hold academic excellence prizes including the Vice-Chancellor's Prize for Best Graduating Student in Engineering (Igbinedion University, 2018).
