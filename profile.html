Biography

Who I Am

I am an Applied AI Researcher and Petroleum & Energy Engineer with over seven years of experience spanning reservoir simulation, data analytics, data engineering, and frontier AI research. My career has followed an unconventional but purposeful arc — from modelling oil reservoirs deep underground to designing intelligent systems for the telecommunications networks of tomorrow.

Currently pursuing my PhD at Sorbonne University / ISEP Paris within the EDITE doctoral school, I work at the intersection of Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) and Large Language Models (LLMs), applying them to one of the most challenging engineering problems of our time: making Open Radio Access Networks (O-RAN) autonomous and self-healing.

My research vision is clear — AI systems must not only perform, they must explain themselves, reason over structured knowledge, and act in ways that engineers can understand and trust. I am a two-time recipient of the prestigious PTDF Overseas Scholarship, awarded for both my MSc (2022) and my ongoing PhD (2025).
